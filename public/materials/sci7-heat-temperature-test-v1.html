1. Temperature measures:

a) total energy transferred between objects b) average kinetic energy of particles in a substance

a) The bathtub has more heat energy because it has more particles

a) windows reflect cold b) cold is not a substance — heat is leaving through the window

a) the particles stop absorbing energy b) the stove runs out of power temporarily

21. Specific heat capacity scenario. (7 marks)

In two Alberta locations on the same day in July: Calgary (inland, near no large water body) reached 35°C. Tofino, BC (coastal, on the Pacific Ocean) reached 22°C. At night: Calgary dropped to 8°C. Tofino only dropped to 16°C.

a) Why does the ocean keep Tofino warmer at night than Calgary? (2 marks)

b) In summer, which location would have warmer beach water — a freshwater reservoir inland or the Pacific Ocean of the same depth at the same latitude? Why? (2 marks)

c) Alberta river water is coldest in late winter/early spring (snowmelt) and warmest in late July/August. Using specific heat capacity, explain this seasonal pattern. (3 marks)

Part D: Extended Response (10 marks)

22. Choose ONE of the following scenarios and write a 5–8 sentence response. Use at least three specific terms from the unit in your answer.

Option A: You are designing a temperature-controlled shipping container that must keep vaccines cold (2–8°C) during transport from Edmonton to remote northern Alberta communities in winter (outside temperature −35°C). Explain the insulation design, which modes of heat transfer you need to address, and any material properties that matter.

Option B: A coal-fired power plant in Alberta draws water from a river, uses it to cool turbines, and returns the warm water to the river at 15°C above the river's natural temperature. Explain the heat transfer concept involved, the ecological harm this causes, and one solution.

Option C: Explain why Alberta has more extreme temperature swings than Vancouver (both at similar latitudes), using the concepts of specific heat capacity, heat transfer, and at least one geographic factor.
