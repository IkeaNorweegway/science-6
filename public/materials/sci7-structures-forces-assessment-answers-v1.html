Part D: Extended Response (10 marks)

Rubric: Structure type identified (1); load type causing failure identified (1); internal force causing deck tearing identified (1); modern design solution described (2); correct use of ≥ 3 unit terms (2); logical organization (1); complete response addressing all 4 points (2)

Sample answer: The Tacoma Narrows Bridge was a suspension bridge — a combination structure with a frame roadway deck supported by cables under tension, anchored to solid concrete blocks. The load that caused the failure was a dynamic load: the wind was moderate in speed but matched the bridge's natural frequency, causing resonance. The deck began to oscillate with growing amplitude — demonstrating torsion (twisting along the long axis of the bridge). As the torsional oscillations grew, the deck experienced shear failure at its connections and finally tore apart. The failure was not caused by exceeding load capacity; it was caused by resonance. Modern suspension bridges prevent this with: open-mesh roadway decks (allow wind to pass through rather than act as a surface), stiffening trusses attached to the deck (increase torsional rigidity), and aerodynamic cross-sections (prevent the lift-and-twist cycle that caused resonance). The central design lesson: dynamic loads from wind require stiffness against torsion, not just tensile strength in the cables.
