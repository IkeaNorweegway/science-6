Part C: Application (15 marks)

20. Read the scenario and answer the questions. (8 marks)

In the 1990s, wolves were removed from an Alberta mountain national park. Within 10 years: elk populations increased dramatically; willows and aspens along riverbanks were heavily overgrazed by elk; beaver populations declined (beavers rely on willows and aspens for food and dam-building); riverbanks eroded significantly without tree roots; the songbird population dropped because there were fewer trees. When wolves were reintroduced in 2010, elk began avoiding riverbanks; vegetation recovered; beaver populations increased; riverbanks stabilized.

a) What is a keystone species? Does the wolf qualify in this scenario? Explain. (3 marks)

b) How did the removal of wolves — a single species — affect four other species? Name each affected species and explain the connection. (4 marks)

c) What concept does this scenario illustrate about biodiversity and ecosystem stability? (1 mark)
