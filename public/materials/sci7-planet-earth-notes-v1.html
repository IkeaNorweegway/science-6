MISCONCEPTION

"Tectonic plates are the same as continents." Why this is wrong: Plates include both continental crust and oceanic crust. Many plate boundaries are   (in the middle of oceans). The North American Plate, for example, includes most of North America AND the western half of the Atlantic Ocean. Some plates are almost entirely oceanic (Pacific Plate). Continents ride on plates; they are not the same thing as plates.

Three subtypes:

"Fossils are mostly large dinosaur bones." Why this is wrong: Most fossils are   — microscopic algae, pollen, bacteria. The vast majority of the fossil record consists of shells, marine invertebrates, plant fragments, and microfossils. Alberta's Badlands fossil sites are remarkable precisely because large dinosaur fossils ARE found there — they are the exception.
