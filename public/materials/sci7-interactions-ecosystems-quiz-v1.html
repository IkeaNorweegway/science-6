Part A: Selected Response (10 marks — 1 mark each)

Circle the best answer.

1. Which is an abiotic factor in an Alberta wetland?

a) beaver b) cattail c) water temperature d) decomposer bacteria

2. Which organism is a primary consumer?

a) wolf b) snowshoe hare c) aspen tree d) mushroom

3. A decomposer is best described as:

a) an organism that produces its own food b) a consumer that eats plants only

c) an organism that breaks down dead material and recycles nutrients d) a top predator

4. In the food chain: grass → deer → wolf → decomposers — what do the arrows represent?

a) the direction predators travel b) the flow of energy from eaten to eater

c) the direction nutrients move d) which organism is bigger

5. Which trophic level has the MOST energy available?

a) tertiary consumers b) secondary consumers c) primary consumers d) producers

6. Using the 10% rule: if producers contain 20,000 units of energy, how much is available to primary consumers?

a) 200 units b) 2,000 units c) 20,000 units d) 200,000 units

7. A food web is more realistic than a food chain because:

a) it shows only the most important species b) it shows all organisms eat and are eaten by multiple species

c) it is simpler to draw d) it only includes producers and consumers

8. What would happen if decomposers were removed from an ecosystem?

a) only bacteria would be affected b) top predators would immediately die

c) nutrients would be locked in dead matter and producers would be starved of minerals

d) nothing — decomposers are not important

9. An energy pyramid shows that:

a) energy increases toward the top b) energy is equal at all levels

c) only about 10% of energy transfers to the next level d) producers have the least energy

10. Why are there fewer wolves than deer in the same forest?

a) wolves need more territory b) wolves eat more food per day than deer

c) less energy is available at higher trophic levels d) wolves have shorter lifespans

a) What trophic level is the owl?  

b) Name one omnivore in the food web if the fox also eats berries (berries = producer level):  

c) If the mouse population collapsed due to disease, what would happen to the owl population and the grass? Explain each.

13. Explain the 10% rule. Why does this rule mean that top predators need large territories? (4 marks)

14. A student argues: "Decomposers are just clean-up crew — the ecosystem would work fine without them." Write a 3-sentence refutation of this argument. Use a specific example. (4 marks)
