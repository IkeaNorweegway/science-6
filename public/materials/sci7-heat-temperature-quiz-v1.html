Part A: Selected Response (10 marks)

Circle the best answer.

1. Temperature measures:

a) the total amount of heat in an object b) the average kinetic energy of particles

c) how quickly heat moves through an object d) the rate of energy transfer

2. 25°C =   K

a) 25 K b) 248 K c) 298 K d) 373 K

3. Absolute zero is:

a) 0°C b) the temperature at which water freezes c) −273°C d) the boiling point of liquid nitrogen

4. A cup of soup at 75°C and a large pot of soup at 75°C are compared. The pot:

a) has the same heat energy as the cup b) has less heat energy because it is cooled by the larger container

c) has more heat energy because it contains more particles d) has a higher temperature than the cup

5. Heat always flows:

a) from less dense to more dense materials b) from cool objects to warm objects

c) from warm objects to cool objects d) in both directions equally

6. "The cold is coming in through the window." This statement is:

a) correct — cold is a real substance that flows in b) incorrect — heat is flowing out through the window

c) correct in winter but not in summer d) incorrect — only radiation moves through windows

7. When a material is heated, its particles:

a) become heavier b) move faster and spread farther apart c) merge into larger particles d) stop moving

8. A railroad track is left without an expansion gap. In summer, the most likely result is:

a) the track cracks from brittleness b) the track buckles because it cannot expand

c) the track contracts and gaps appear d) no effect

9. When a substance is melting, its temperature:

a) rises steadily b) drops c) stays constant while the change of state occurs d) fluctuates randomly

10. Why does the liquid rise in a thermometer when placed in hot water?

a) the liquid becomes heavier b) the liquid expands as particles speed up and spread apart

c) heat pushes the liquid upward d) air pressure pushes the liquid up
