Part G: ★ Challenge

24. You are a geologist visiting an outcrop in the Alberta Badlands. You find: (8 marks)

  • The bottom layer (Layer 1) is limestone containing marine invertebrate shells
  • Above it (Layer 2) is a thick layer of sandstone with no fossils
  • Above that (Layer 3) is shale containing large dinosaur bones and teeth
  • At the very top (Layer 4) is a thin clay-rich layer with unusually high concentrations of the element iridium (rare on Earth, but common in meteorites)

a. Using the law of superposition, list the layers from oldest to youngest.

b. What type of environment does the limestone (Layer 1) suggest was here in the past?

c. Layer 3 contains Cretaceous dinosaur fossils. What does this tell you about what Alberta looked like during that era?

d. Layer 4's iridium is found in rock layers worldwide at the same geological moment. What event does scientists believe this represents, and what happened to the organisms in Layer 3 shortly after?

e. All four rock layers are sedimentary. What does this tell you about Alberta's geological environment over this time period?
