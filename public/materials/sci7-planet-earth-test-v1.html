a) it has cooled over billions of years b) extreme pressure keeps it solid

a) igneous rock b) metamorphic rock c) sedimentary rock d) mantle rock

a) a volcano erupted repeatedly in the same location b) oceanic plates pushed under North America and compressed the crust

a) the focus is on the surface; the epicenter is underground b) the focus is underground; the epicenter is on the surface directly above

c) they are the same thing d) the focus measures size; the epicenter measures shaking

a) it is located far from plate boundaries b) its lava has low viscosity and releases gas gradually

a) chemical weathering b) erosion c) mechanical weathering d) deposition

a) wind erosion b) river erosion c) glaciation — ice sheets that covered Alberta and deposited till

a) they are the largest fossils found b) they allow correlation of rock layers across distant locations

a) volcanic rock from ancient eruptions b) metamorphic rock from tectonic pressure

20. Alberta Geology application. (8 marks)

A geologist is studying a rock outcrop in the Drumheller Badlands. She finds (from bottom to top):

  • Layer 1: Limestone with marine invertebrate fossils
  • Layer 2: Thick sandstone with no fossils
  • Layer 3: Shale with dinosaur bones, fish scales, and small mammal teeth
  • Layer 4: A thin, dark clay layer rich in iridium (an element rare on Earth but common in meteorites)
  • Layer 5: Sandstone with only small mammal fossils; no dinosaurs

a) Using the law of superposition, which layer is oldest? Which is most recent? (1 mark)
