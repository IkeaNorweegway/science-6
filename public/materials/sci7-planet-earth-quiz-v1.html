Part A: Selected Response (10 marks)

Circle the best answer.

1. Earth's layers from outermost to innermost are:

a) core, mantle, crust, inner core b) crust, mantle, outer core, inner core

c) crust, outer core, mantle, inner core d) mantle, crust, core, inner core

2. Earth's magnetic field is generated by:

a) the solid inner core spinning b) convection in the liquid outer core

c) tectonic plate movement d) the mantle's radioactive decay

3. Scientists know what Earth's interior looks like by studying:

a) deep drill holes b) volcanic lava samples c) seismic waves d) satellite images

4. Which rock type forms when magma cools slowly underground?

a) extrusive igneous b) intrusive igneous c) sedimentary d) metamorphic

5. Fossils are most commonly found in:

a) igneous rock b) metamorphic rock c) sedimentary rock d) all rock types equally

6. Metamorphic rock forms when:

a) lava cools at the surface b) sediments are compressed and cemented

c) existing rock is transformed by heat and pressure without melting d) minerals precipitate from water

7. The theory of plate tectonics states that:

a) the continents have always been in their current positions b) Earth's lithosphere is broken into moving plates

c) volcanic activity occurs everywhere equally d) mountains form only near oceans

8. Which type of plate boundary produces mid-ocean ridges and new seafloor?

a) convergent b) divergent c) transform d) subduction

9. Where two continental plates collide, the result is:

a) one plate subducts and a trench forms b) both plates crumple and a mountain chain forms

c) a rift valley opens between them d) volcanic island arcs form

10. Which evidence most directly shows that the continents have moved over time?

a) volcanoes are near the ocean b) the same species fossils appear on continents now separated by oceans

c) all rocks are the same age d) earthquakes happen every day
