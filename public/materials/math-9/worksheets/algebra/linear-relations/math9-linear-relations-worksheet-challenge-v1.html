2. Two lines share the same y-intercept (b = 4) but have slopes 2 and −2.

4. Alberta context — two competing services.

A Calgary plumber charges $50 call-out + $70/hour. A second plumber charges $110 call-out + $50/hour.

(a) Write equations for both. State what type of variation each is.

(b) For what number of hours do they charge the same? Show algebraically.

(c) For a 3-hour job, which is cheaper? By how much?

(d) Interpret the y-intercepts and slopes in context — what does each number mean for a homeowner?

7. Rate of change reasoning.

A water tank holds 1200 L and is being drained at a constant rate. After 10 minutes, 850 L remain.

(a) Find the rate of change (L/min) and write the equation for volume V at time t.

(b) When will the tank be empty? Solve algebraically and interpret.

(c) Is this direct or partial variation? What does the y-intercept represent?

(d) What does it mean that the slope is negative in this context?

8. Open-ended — matching slopes.

The slope of a line is −3/5.

(a) Write two points that could be on this line. (Do not use the equation — just use the slope definition.)

(b) Write the equation of a second line parallel to this one (same slope) that passes through (10, 0).

(c) Write the equation of a line perpendicular to this line. (Hint: perpendicular slopes are negative reciprocals.) What slope would that be?
