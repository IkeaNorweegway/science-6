Teacher Answer Key — Not for Distribution

Cover the answer. Try the question. Then check.

1. Pool: 200 L/min, starts with 50 L

  • Verbal: Starting at 50 L, increasing by 200 L per minute.
  • Table:
Time (min)01234
Volume (L)50250450650850
  • Equation: V = 200t + 50
  • Type: Partial variation — starts at 50 (non-zero when t=0); the y-intercept is the initial 50 L already in the pool.
Common Mistake

Forgetting the initial 50 L and writing V = 200t.

2. Slope from tables

(a) x:0,1,2,3 | y:4,7,10,13

Change in y = 7−4 = 3 for change in x = 1.

m = 3 — Positive slope

(b) x:0,2,4,6 | y:12,8,4,0

Change in y = 8−12 = −4 for change in x = 2.

m = −4/2 = −2 — Negative slope

3. Slope from two points

(a) (−2,1) and (4,13)

m = (13 − 1) / (4 − (−2)) = 12/6 = 2

(b) (3,7) and (3,−2)

m = (−2−7)/(3−3) = −9/0 = undefined — vertical line (both points have the same x-value)

(c) (0,5) and (8,5)

m = (5−5)/(8−0) = 0/8 = 0 — horizontal line (y doesn't change)

4. Slope and y-intercept from equation

(a) y = 2x − 3: m = 2, b = −3

y-intercept point: (0, −3) | Second point: (1, −1) [−3 + 2 = −1]
(Graph: starts at (0,−3), rises 2 for every 1 right)

(b) y = −x + 4: m = −1, b = 4

(c) y = 5: m = 0, b = 5 — This line is a horizontal line at y = 5 — it runs parallel to the x-axis at height 5.

5. Write the equation

(a) Slope 3, through (0, −2):

y-intercept is given (through origin of y-axis at −2). y = 3x − 2

(b) Slope −2, through (1, 5):

5 = −2(1) + b → 5 = −2 + b → b = 7 → y = −2x + 7

(c) Through (0, 4) and (3, 10):

m = (10−4)/(3−0) = 6/3 = 2. y-int = 4. y = 2x + 4

6. Direct or partial variation

(a) Car: 8 L per 100 km, 0 fuel at 0 km:

Direct variation — starts at 0 when km = 0. Equation: F = 0.08d (or F = 8d/100)

(b) Storage unit: $50 + $25/month:

Partial variation — b = 50 ≠ 0. Equation: C = 25m + 50

(c) x:0,1,2,3 | y:0,6,12,18:

y = 0 when x = 0 → passes through origin. Direct variation. Equation: y = 6x

7. Alberta context — Calgary food truck

(a) Revenue per meal = $8. Fixed costs = $120.

P = 8m − 120

(b) Break-even (P = 0):

0 = 8m − 120 → 8m = 120 → m = 15 meals

(c) 30 meals:

P = 8(30) − 120 = 240 − 120 = $120 profit

8. Both intercepts

(a) y = 3x − 9:

x-intercept (y=0): 0 = 3x−9 → x = 3 → point: (3, 0)

y-intercept (x=0): y = −9 → point: (0, −9)

(b) y = −2x + 8:

x-intercept: 0 = −2x+8 → x = 4 → (4, 0)

y-intercept: (0, 8)

9. Rate of change — cyclist

(a) Rate of change:

m = (120 − 48) / (5 − 2) = 72 / 3 = 24 km/h

(b) Direct variation?

At t=0: d=24(0)=0. Origin is included — she starts at Edmonton (distance 0). Yes — direct variation. The ratio d/t = 24 is constant.

(c) d = 24t

10. Two parallel lines: y = 2x + 3 and y = 2x − 5

Both have slope 2 → parallel lines (never intersect).

Different y-intercepts (3 and −5) → the line y=2x+3 is 8 units above y=2x−5 at every x value.

On a graph: two identical-looking lines, one shifted 8 units downward.

Common Mistake

Thinking same slope means the lines are the same line — they must also share the same y-intercept for that to be true.
