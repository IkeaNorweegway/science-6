Key Rules

Adding

Remove brackets, collect like terms.

(3x² + 2x) + (x² − 5x) = 4x² − 3x

Subtracting

Distribute the negative sign to EVERY term in the second bracket, then collect.

(3x² + 2x) − (x² − 5x) = 3x² + 2x − x² + 5x = 2x² + 7x

Multiplying — Monomial × Polynomial

Multiply the monomial by every term inside.

3x(2x² − x + 4) = 6x³ − 3x² + 12x

Multiplying Powers

xm × xn = x(m+n)    →    x × x = x² (NOT 2x)

Multiplying — Binomial × Binomial

Every term in the first bracket multiplies every term in the second (4 partial products). Use the area model.

Worked Example 1 — Subtracting Polynomials

Find: (4x² + 3x − 1) − (2x² − x + 6)

Distribute the negative: 4x² + 3x − 1 − 2x² + x − 6

Collect: (4−2)x² + (3+1)x + (−1−6) = 2x² + 4x − 7

The most common error: only flipping the sign on the first term inside the bracket.

Worked Example 2 — Monomial × Polynomial

Expand: −2x(x² − 3x + 5)

−2x × x² = −2x³

−2x × (−3x) = +6x²

−2x × 5 = −10x

Result: −2x³ + 6x² − 10x
