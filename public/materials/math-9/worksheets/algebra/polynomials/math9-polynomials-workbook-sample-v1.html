Sample document. This demonstrates the workbook format applied to Grade 9 Polynomials. A full unit workbook would include ~30 questions, additional visual tasks, and a teacher copy with lesson-release markers and misconception target labels.

About this workbook: Questions are mixed up on purpose. You will not know which polynomial skill each question is testing until you read it. Figuring that out is part of the work.

Stretch Zone

Attempt only after completing all earlier questions. Each one connects two or more ideas.

S1 ★★
A hockey rink in Edmonton has a rectangular playing surface with length (3x + 8) m and width (2x + 5) m. Around it is a uniform concrete border 2 m wide.
(a) Simplified polynomial for the area of the playing surface.
(b) Simplified polynomial for the total area (surface + border).
(c) Expression for the border area alone. What degree is it, and what does that tell you?
S2 ★★

Two polynomials add to give (5x² − 2x + 3). One of them is (2x² + x − 1).

(a) Find the other polynomial. Show your method.
(b) Check your answer by adding the two polynomials together.
(c) What operation did you use to find the missing polynomial? Explain in one sentence why that works.
S3 ★★
A student claims (x + 2)(x + 3) and x² + 6 are the same expression, because "you just multiply the numbers."
(a) Expand (x + 2)(x + 3) correctly using the area model or distribution.
(b) Evaluate both expressions when x = 1. Are they equal?
(c) Explain in 2–3 sentences what error the student made, and why the area model shows their reasoning is wrong.
