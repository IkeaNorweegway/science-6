Solving Equations — Step Order

Six Steps
  1. Distribute — expand any brackets
  2. Combine like terms on the same side
  3. Move variables to one side (add/subtract)
  4. Move constants to the other side
  5. Divide to isolate the variable
  6. Verify by substituting back

To clear fractions: Multiply every term by the LCD before doing anything else.

Inequalities — The One New Rule

The Sign-Flip Rule

Solving works identically to equations — EXCEPT:

When you multiply or divide both sides by a negative number, flip the inequality sign.

This is the only difference. All other steps are the same.

Worked Example 1 — Multi-Step Equation

Solve: 4(x − 3) = 2x + 6

Distribute: 4x − 12 = 2x + 6

Move variables: 2x − 12 = 6

Move constants: 2x = 18

Divide: x = 9

Verify: 4(9 − 3) = 4(6) = 24    2(9) + 6 = 24 ✓

Worked Example 2 — Rational Coefficients

Solve: x/3 + 1/4 = 5/12

LCD of 3, 4, 12 = 12

Multiply all terms by 12: 4x + 3 = 5

Solve: 4x = 2 → x = 1/2

Worked Example 3 — Inequality (Sign Flip)

Solve: −3x + 6 ≤ 0

−3x ≤ −6

Divide by −3 and FLIP: x ≥ 2
