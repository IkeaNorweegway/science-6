Q4 ★ — Slope from two points

(a) (2,5) and (6,13):
m = (13−5)/(6−2) = 8/4 = 2 — Positive

(b) (−1,4) and (3,−4):
m = (−4−4)/(3−(−1)) = −8/4 = −2 — Negative

(c) (0,3) and (5,3):
m = (3−3)/(5−0) = 0 — Zero (horizontal line at y = 3)

Q5 ★ — Adding and subtracting polynomials

(a) Total length:
(3x2+2x−1)+(x2−5x+4) = 4x2+(2x−5x)+(−1+4) = 4x2−3x+3

(b) Difference (first minus second):
(3x2+2x−1)−(x2−5x+4) = 3x2+2x−1−x2+5x−4 = 2x2+7x−5

Q6 ★ — Inequalities

Q7 ★★ — Error analysis: y = 3x

(a) Error:
The student confused the slope (3, the coefficient of x) with the y-intercept. In y=3x, the y-intercept is 0 (no constant term), not 3. The slope is 3.

(b) Side-by-side tables:

Q9 ★ — Multi-step equations

(a) 2(x+5) = 18:
Distribute: 2x + 10 = 18 → 2x = 8 → x = 4
Verify: 2(4+5) = 2(9) = 18 ✓

(b) 3x − 1 = x + 7:
Move variables: 2x = 8 → x = 4
Verify: 3(4)−1=11; 4+7=11 ✓

(c) −4(x−3) = 2x + 6:
−4x+12 = 2x+6 → 6 = 6x → x = 1
Verify: −4(1−3)=−4(−2)=8; 2(1)+6=8 ✓

Q21 ★★ — Special cases

(a) 3x+6=3(x+2) → 3x+6=3x+6 → 0=0. Infinitely many solutions — identity (always true).

(b) 2x+5=2x−3 → 5=−3. No solution — contradiction (never true).

Q22 ★★ — Connecting slope and equation

(a) Line 1: m=(13−1)/(4−(−2))=12/6=2. b: 1=2(−2)+b → b=5. y=2x+5

(b) Same slope AND same y-intercept. They are the same line.

(c) Two lines with the same slope are parallel and do not cross because they have the same steepness but (normally) different y-intercepts. Here, because they share both slope and y-intercept, they are identical — every point is an intersection.

Stretch Zone

S1 — Two companies

(a) Company 1: C=25h+40 (partial). Company 2: C=35h (direct).
(b) Company 1 cheaper: 25h+40 < 35h → 40 < 10h → h > 4 — for more than 4 hours, Company 1 is cheaper.
(c) At h=4: both charge $140 exactly — neither is more expensive at 4 hours.

S2 — Rectangle area = 26

(a) Area = (3x+2)(x−1) = 3x2−x−2
(b) 3x2−x−28=0. Discriminant = 1+4(3)(28)=337 — not a perfect square, no integer solution.
Try x=3: 27−3−28=−4 ≠ 0. Try x=4: 48−4−28=16 ≠ 0.
(Note: the equation has irrational roots ≈ 3.25 and −2.92.)
(c) With x>0: approximately x ≈ 3.25. This is a limitation of the problem — the worksheet is intended as an exploratory stretch, not requiring an exact integer answer.

S3 — Inequality sign flip error

(a) Error: student divided by −3 but forgot to flip the inequality sign. Dividing by a negative always reverses direction.
(b) Correct: −3x+6>12 → −3x>6 → x<−2 (open circle at −2, arrow left)
(c) Verify x=−3 (in solution): −3(−3)+6=9+6=15>12 ✓. Verify x=0 (not in solution): 0+6=6>12? No ✓ (correctly excluded).
