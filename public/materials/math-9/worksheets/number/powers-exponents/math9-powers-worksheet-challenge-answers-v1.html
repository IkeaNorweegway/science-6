Teacher Answer Key — Not for Distribution

Cover the answer. Try the question. Then check.

Format note: Setup and decision step shown. Final answer listed. Full execution left to student to verify.

1. Error analysis

(a) (5x2y)3 = 5x6y3

Error named: Forgot to apply the exponent to the coefficient 5. The power of a product rule applies to ALL factors inside the bracket — including numerical coefficients.

Setup: (5x2y)3 = 53 × (x2)3 × y3 = 125 × x6 × y3

Final answer: 125x6y3

(b) a4 × a5 = a20

Error named: Used the power-of-a-power rule (multiplied exponents) instead of the product-of-powers rule (add exponents). These are different operations.

Setup: Same base → add exponents: a(4+5)

Final answer: a9

(c) (4+x)3 = 64+x3

Error named: Distributed the exponent over addition. Exponents only distribute over multiplication and division — never over addition or subtraction.

What DOES distribute: (4x)3 = 43x3 = 64x3 (multiplication inside bracket). For addition, full expansion is required: (4+x)3 = 64 + 48x + 12x2 + x3.

Final answer: Cannot be simplified by distributing — full expansion required.

(d) (−3)2 = −9

Error named: Applied the negative sign as if the even power doesn't remove it. Any even power of a negative base is positive.

Setup: (−3)2 = (−3)×(−3). Negative × negative = positive.

Final answer: +9

2. Explain the difference

(a) 23 × 24 vs. (23)4

23 × 24: two separate groups of factors being combined → ADD exponents → 27
(23)4: the power 23 is itself raised to a power → MULTIPLY exponents → 212

Why the difference: In a product, the exponents count two separate sets of 2s joined together. In a power of a power, the outer exponent tells how many copies of the entire inner power you have — n copies of m factors each = m×n total factors.

Values: 27 = 128 vs. 212 = 4,096

(b) −34 vs. (−3)4

−34: base is 3 (brackets absent). Exponent applies to 3 first, then negate: −(34) = −81
(−3)4: base is −3 (brackets enclose the negative). Exponent applies to the whole −3: positive because even exponent.

Values: −34 = −81 vs. (−3)4 = +81

3. Simplify completely — positive exponents only

(a) (2x3y−2)4 ÷ (8x2y)2

Setup — numerator: 24 × x12 × y−8 = 16x12y−8
Setup — denominator: 82 × x4 × y2 = 64x4y2
Divide: (16/64) × x(12−4) × y(−8−2) = (1/4)x8y−10

Final answer: x8/(4y10)

(b) [(−3a2b)3 × (ab2)4] ÷ (−3a5b6)

Setup — (−3a2b)3: (−3)3a6b3 = −27a6b3
Setup — (ab2)4: a4b8
Product: −27a10b11
Divide by (−3a5b6): (−27/−3) × a(10−5) × b(11−6) = 9a5b5

Final answer: 9a5b5

(c) (2−1 + 4−1)2

Inside bracket first (do NOT distribute the exponent over the sum):
2−1 = 1/2 | 4−1 = 1/4
Sum: 1/2 + 1/4 = 3/4
Squared: (3/4)2 = 9/16

4. 2100 vs. 1002

1002 = 10,000
210 = 1,024 < 10,000. But 213 = 8,192 and 214 = 16,384 > 10,000.
So 2100 >> 214 >> 10,000 = 1002.

2100 > 1002 — by an enormous margin. Exponential growth with a fixed base far outpaces polynomial growth for large enough exponents.

5. Find the value of n

(a) 2n = 128

Setup: Express 128 as a power of 2: 27 = 128

n = 7

(b) 3n × 32 = 38

Setup: Product of powers → add: n + 2 = 8

n = 6

(c) (5n)3 = 512

Setup: Power of power → multiply: 3n = 12

n = 4

(d) 4n = 1/16

Setup: 1/16 = 4−2 (since 42 = 16)

n = −2

6. Counter-argument: a0 = 0

Using the quotient rule:
Take any specific example: 54 ÷ 54 = 625 ÷ 625 = 1 (any nonzero number divided by itself equals 1).
By the quotient rule: 54 ÷ 54 = 5(4−4) = 50.
Therefore 50 = 1, not 0.

The student's claim "no power used means zero" misunderstands what an exponent does — an exponent counts repeated multiplication, it does not get multiplied into the base. There is no multiplication by zero anywhere in this expression.

7. Multi-step

(a) (4x5)3 ÷ (2x2)4 × 2x

(4x5)3 = 43 × x15 = 64x15
(2x2)4 = 24 × x8 = 16x8
÷: 64x15 ÷ 16x8 = 4x7
× 2x: 4x7 × 2x = 8x8

(b) [(a2b−1)3]2 ÷ (a3b)2

Inner power: (a2b−1)3 = a6b−3
Outer power: (a6b−3)2 = a12b−6
Denominator: (a3b)2 = a6b2
Divide: a(12−6) × b(−6−2) = a6b−8

Final answer: a6/b8

8. Design a problem — model answer

(Accept any expression using at least three different laws that correctly simplifies to 4x3.)

Sample expression: (2x2)3 ÷ (x0 × 2x3)

Simplification:
Numerator: (2x2)3 = 8x6 (power of product)
Denominator: x0 × 2x3 = 1 × 2x3 = 2x3 (zero exponent + product)
Quotient: 8x6 ÷ 2x3 = 4x3 (quotient of powers)

Laws used: Power of a product, zero exponent, quotient of powers ✓
