Common Mistake

−24 and (−2)4 are NOT the same. −24 = −16; (−2)4 = +16. The brackets determine the base.

2. Zero and negative exponents

(a) 80 = 1 | (−5)0 = 1 | (xy)0 = 1 (any nonzero quantity raised to 0 = 1)

(b) 4−2 = 1/42 = 1/16

(c) (1/3)−2 = (3/1)2 = 9 (negative exponent flips the fraction)

(d) (−2)−3 = 1/(−2)3 = 1/(−8) = −1/8

Common Mistake

The result in (d) is negative because the BASE is negative (−2), not because of the negative exponent. The negative exponent alone never creates a negative result.

9. Evaluate using exponent rules

(a) (24 × 2−2)3

Inside bracket first: 2(4+(−2)) = 22. Then (22)3 = 2(2×3) = 26 = 64

(b) (32 ÷ 34) × 33

3(2−4) = 3−2. Then 3−2 × 33 = 3(−2+3) = 31 = 3

(c) (−1)202

202 is even → 1
