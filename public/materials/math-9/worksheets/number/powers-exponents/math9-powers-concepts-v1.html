Sign Rule for Negative Bases
  • Even exponent → positive result: (−2)4 = +16
  • Odd exponent → negative result: (−2)3 = −8

−34 ≠ (−3)4   −34 = −81 (base is 3)   (−3)4 = +81 (base is −3)

Worked Example 1 — Product and quotient laws

Simplify: x5 × x3 ÷ x2

x5 × x3 = x(5+3) = x8    ← product rule (add exponents)

x8 ÷ x2 = x(8−2) = x6    ← quotient rule (subtract exponents)

Why add when multiplying? Expanded: x5 × x3 = (x·x·x·x·x)(x·x·x) = x8. Counting factors.

Worked Example 2 — Combined laws

Simplify: (3x2)4 ÷ (9x3)

Step 1 — Power of product: (3x2)4 = 34 × (x2)4 = 81x8

Step 2 — Divide: 81x8 ÷ 9x3 = (81/9) × x(8−3) = 9x5

Worked Example 3 — Negative exponents

Evaluate: (−2)−3

(−2)−3 = 1/(−2)3 = 1/(−8) = −1/8

Key: The negative exponent says "move to denominator." The base's sign still matters.
