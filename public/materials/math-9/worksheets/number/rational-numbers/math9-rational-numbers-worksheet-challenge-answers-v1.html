Teacher Answer Key — Not for Distribution

Cover the answer. Try the question. Then check.

Format note: Setup and decision step are shown. Final answer is listed. Full execution is left to the student to verify.

1. Error analysis

(a) 5/6 − 1/4 = (5−1)/(6−4) = 4/2 = 2

Error named: Subtracted numerators and denominators separately — not a valid operation for fraction subtraction.

Setup: Find LCD of 6 and 4 = 12. Rewrite: 5/6 = 10/12 and 1/4 = 3/12. Then subtract numerators.

Final answer: 7/12

(b) (−3/4) ÷ (3/8) = (−3/4) × (3/8) = −9/32

Error named: Did not take the reciprocal of the divisor — multiplied by the divisor instead of its reciprocal.

Setup: (−3/4) ÷ (3/8) = (−3/4) × (8/3). Sign: neg × pos = negative.

Final answer: −2

(c) (−2/3)2 = −4/9

Error named: Treated the negative sign as surviving the squaring — even powers always produce a positive result regardless of the sign of the base.

Setup: (−2/3)2 = (−2/3) × (−2/3). neg × neg = positive.

Final answer: 4/9

2. "Dividing by a fraction gives a larger result."

True when: The divisor is a proper fraction (between 0 and 1 exclusive).

Example: 6 ÷ (1/2) = 12 → larger than 6 ✓

False when: The divisor is greater than 1 (improper fraction or whole number > 1).

Example: 6 ÷ (3/2) = 4 → smaller than 6 ✗

Condition: The claim holds whenever the divisor is strictly between 0 and 1. When the divisor is greater than 1, the quotient is smaller. When the divisor equals 1, the quotient is unchanged.

3. −3/4 vs. −(3/4)

They are the same number. −3/4, −(3/4), and 3/(−4) are the three equivalent sign forms of the same rational number.

Number line: all three sit at the same point — 3/4 of a unit to the left of zero.

Calculation: −3/4 = −0.75 and −(3/4) = −0.75. Equal ✓

The student's claim is false. The placement of the negative sign in the notation is a convention; it does not create a different number.

4. [(−2/3 + 1/2)2 − 1/4] ÷ (−3/8) — full step explanation

Step 1 — innermost bracket: −2/3 + 1/2. LCD = 6: −4/6 + 3/6 = −1/6

Step 2 — exponent: (−1/6)2 = (−1/6) × (−1/6) = 1/36 (neg × neg = pos)

Step 3 — subtraction inside outer bracket: 1/36 − 1/4. LCD = 36: 1/36 − 9/36 = −8/36 = −2/9

Step 4 — division: (−2/9) ÷ (−3/8) = (−2/9) × (−8/3). neg × neg = positive. = 16/27

Final answer: 16/27

5. Find x

(a) 3/4 × x = −9/8

Setup: Isolate x by dividing both sides by 3/4 (i.e., multiply by 4/3).
x = (−9/8) × (4/3)

Final answer: x = −3/2

(b) x ÷ (−2/3) = 9/4

Setup: Isolate x by multiplying both sides by −2/3.
x = (9/4) × (−2/3)

Final answer: x = −3/2

(c) (x + 1/2) × (−4/3) = 2

Setup: First divide both sides by −4/3 (multiply by −3/4):
x + 1/2 = 2 × (−3/4) = −3/2
Then subtract 1/2: x = −3/2 − 1/2

Final answer: x = −2

6. Alberta context — cabin costs

(a) Family C's share

Setup: 1 − 1/3 − 5/12. LCD = 12: 12/12 − 4/12 − 5/12

Family C: 1/4

(b) Payments on $780

Setup: Multiply total by each fraction.

Family A: 780 × 1/3 = $260
Family B: 780 × 5/12 = $325
Family C: 780 × 1/4 = $195

(c) Family B reduces to 1/4

Setup: New B payment = $780 × 1/4 = $195. Change = $325 − $195 = $130 reduction.

Family B's payment decreases by $130. The remaining families must absorb this increase — any valid allocation that sums to $130 is acceptable.

7. Explaining division by 1/3

Model explanation: Dividing by 1/3 asks "how many one-third pieces fit into this number?" Since each whole contains three thirds, any number n contains 3n thirds. Counting those thirds gives the same result as multiplying by 3. The two operations are equivalent because the reciprocal of 1/3 is 3/1 = 3.

Numerical example: 6 ÷ 1/3 = 18 and 6 × 3 = 18. Same ✓

8. Design a problem — model answer

(Accept any valid problem whose solution equals −5/6 and uses at least two operations.)

Sample problem: A scuba diver descends at −5/12 m/s for 4 seconds, then rises 5/6 m. What is the diver's net change in depth?

Solution:
Descent: (−5/12) × 4 = −20/12 = −5/3
Net: −5/3 + 5/6 = −10/6 + 5/6 = −5/6 ✓
