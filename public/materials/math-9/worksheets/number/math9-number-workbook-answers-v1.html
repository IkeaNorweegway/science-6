Q1 ★ — Ordering rational numbers

Converting to decimals for comparison: −5/6 ≈ −0.833 | −3/4 = −0.750 | −1/3 ≈ −0.333 | 0 | 1/2 = 0.500

Least → greatest: −5/6, −3/4, −1/3, 0, 1/2

Number line: −5/6 left of −3/4 (past −0.75) | −1/3 just left of 0 | 0 at centre | 1/2 right of centre

Why −3/4 < −1/3: When comparing negative fractions, the one that is farther from zero (larger in absolute value) has a smaller value because it is more negative — it sits further to the left on the number line.

Q6 ★ — Estimating square roots

(a) √38:
62 = 36 < 38 < 49 = 72. So 6 < √38 < 7

Q7 ★★ — Adding rationals: error analysis

(a) Error named:
Student added numerators (3 + (−5) = −2) and denominators (4 + 6 = 10) separately. This is not valid — denominators tell the size of the pieces; you cannot add pieces of different sizes by just summing them.
Rule broken: Must find a common denominator before adding fractions.

(b) Correct answer:
LCD of 4 and 6 = 12. 3/4 = 9/12; −5/6 = −10/12
9/12 + (−10/12) = −1/12

(c) Explain to a classmate:
When adding fractions, find a common denominator first so the pieces are the same size. Then add only the numerators. Here: 3/4 = 9/12 and −5/6 = −10/12, so the answer is 9/12 − 10/12 = −1/12.

(a) (−3/4) × (2/5):
Sign decision: neg × pos = negative
= −6/20 = −3/10

(b) (−7/8) × (−4/3) — cross-cancel:
Sign: neg × neg = positive
Cancel 4 and 8 (÷4): (−7/2) × (−1/3) = 7/6
= 7/6

(c) (−5/6)2:
= (−5/6) × (−5/6). neg × neg = positive
= 25/36

(b) √0.36:

Q14 ★★ — Quotient and power of power: error analysis

(a) First student — (x3)2 ÷ x4 = x2:
(x3)2 = x6 (power of power: 3×2) → x6 ÷ x4 = x2 (quotient: 6−4)
Answer x2 is correct ✓

(b) Second student — 32 × 34 = 38:
Error: multiplied exponents for a product (should add). Correct: 32 × 34 = 36 = 729

(c) When to add vs. multiply:
ADD exponents when multiplying two powers with the same base (separate groups joined).
MULTIPLY exponents when raising a power to another power (nested exponents).

Q19 ★★ — Dividing by a fraction: error analysis

Example 1: 6 ÷ (1/2) = 12 (answer LARGER than 6) ✓
Example 2: 4 ÷ (1/4) = 16 (answer LARGER than 4) ✓

When dividing by a fraction less than 1, the result is larger — you're asking "how many of this small piece fit?" Since each piece is smaller than 1, more pieces fit than the original amount. The "pizza" analogy fails because division by a fraction is not the same as cutting a pizza.
