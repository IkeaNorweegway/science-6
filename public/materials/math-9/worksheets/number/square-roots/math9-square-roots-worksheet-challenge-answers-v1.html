Square Roots and Radicals

Challenge ★★ — Answer Key

Teacher Answer Key — Not for Distribution

Cover the answer. Try the question. Then check.

Format note: Setup and decision step shown. Final answer listed. Full execution left to student to verify.

1. Error analysis

(a) "√(25+144) = √25 + √144 = 5 + 12 = 17"

Error named: Distributed the radical over addition — radicals do NOT distribute over addition or subtraction.

Setup: Simplify inside the radical first: 25 + 144 = 169. Then take the square root.

Final answer: √169 = 13

(b) "The square roots of 36 are ±6, so √36 = ±6"

Error named: Confused "all square roots of 36" with the principal square root. The ± applies when solving x2 = 36, but the radical symbol √ always means the principal (positive) root only.

Correct: The square roots of 36 are indeed ±6 — but √36 = 6 (positive only). If both roots are intended, write ±√36.

(c) "√50 ≈ 25 because 50 ÷ 2 = 25"

Error named: Confused "divide by 2" with "take the square root" — these are completely different operations.

Setup: √50 lies between 7 (72=49) and 8 (82=64). Since 50 is only 1 above 49, it's very close to 7.

Final answer: √50 ≈ 7.1 (more precisely 7.071...)

2. Prove or disprove

(a) √(ab) = √a × √b — test with a=4, b=9

Left: √(4×9) = √36 = 6
Right: √4 × √9 = 2 × 3 = 6
Equal? Yes → Rule WORKS (radicals distribute over multiplication)

(b) √(a+b) = √a + √b — test with a=9, b=16

Left: √(9+16) = √25 = 5
Right: √9 + √16 = 3 + 4 = 7
Equal? No → Rule does NOT work (radicals do not distribute over addition)

(c) √(a/b) = √a/√b — test with a=49, b=25

Left: √(49/25) = 7/5
Right: √49/√25 = 7/5 = 7/5
Equal? Yes → Rule WORKS (radicals distribute over division)

3. Square with area 50 cm2

(a) Exact side length

Setup: s2 = 50, so s = √50. Factor: √50 = √(25×2) = √25 × √2

Side = 5√2 cm

(b) Exact perimeter

Setup: Perimeter = 4 × side

Perimeter = 4 × 5√2 = 20√2 cm

(c) Rational or irrational? Physical meaning?

Irrational. √2 cannot be expressed as any fraction. Physically: a ruler can only display finitely many decimal places — you can get arbitrarily close to 5√2 ≈ 7.071 cm, but you can never mark the exact length. Any real measurement is only an approximation.

4. Pythagorean triple challenge

(a) Verify (3, 4, 5)

32 + 42 = 9 + 16 = 25 = 52 ✓

(b) Verify (9, 12, 15)

92 + 122 = 81 + 144 = 225 = 152 ✓ (this is 3 × (3,4,5))

(c) Verify (5, 12, 13) and find two scaled triples

52 + 122 = 25 + 144 = 169 = 132 ✓

Scaled triples: (10, 24, 26) [×2] and (15, 36, 39) [×3]

(d) Consecutive-integer right triangles

3,4,5: differences are 4−3=1 and 5−4=1. Yes — these ARE consecutive integers.

For a general (n, n+1, n+2) triple: n2 + (n+1)2 = (n+2)2 → n2−2n−3 = 0 → (n−3)(n+1) = 0 → n = 3.

(3, 4, 5) is the only Pythagorean triple of three consecutive integers.

5. Rope and pole problem

(a) Height of pole (rope 10 m, ground 6 m)

Setup: h2 + 62 = 102. Isolate h2: h2 = 100 − 36 = 64

h = 8 m

(b) Rope lengthened to 12 m — change in height

Setup: h2 + 62 = 122. h2 = 144 − 36 = 108. h = √108 = 6√3 ≈ 10.39 m

New height: 6√3 ≈ 10.4 m
Change: ≈ 10.4 − 8 = 2.4 m — more than 2 m

Why more than 2 m: the rope length and height don't have a linear relationship (it follows the Pythagorean theorem). When the rope becomes more vertical, adding length to the rope gives a disproportionately large gain in height.

7. Alberta context — Banff lodge roof

(a) Slant length from apex to base end

Setup: Height bisects base → half-base = 6 m. Slant2 = 52 + 62

Slant = √(25 + 36) = √61 m

(b) Rational or irrational?

Irrational — 61 is not a perfect square (72 = 49, 82 = 64)

(c) Area of one slant surface

Setup: Area = slant length × depth

Area = √61 × 8 = 8√61 ≈ 62.5 m2

8. Design a problem — model answer

(Accept any valid problem set in an Alberta context, requiring two steps, with an irrational hypotenuse.)

Sample problem: A square flower garden in Camrose has sides of 7 m. A gravel path runs diagonally from one corner to the opposite corner. What is the length of the path?

Solution:
The diagonal splits the square into two right triangles with legs 7 m and 7 m.
d2 = 72 + 72 = 49 + 49 = 98
d = √98 = 7√2 ≈ 9.9 m (irrational) ✓
