Line of Best Fit — Rules

Rules
  1. Draw through the middle of the data (equal points above and below)
  2. Follows the direction of the trend
  3. Does NOT need to pass through the origin or any specific data point
  4. Choose two points on the line (not data points) to find the equation

Finding the equation:
Slope: m = (y2 − y1)/(x2 − x1) using two points on the line.
Then substitute one point into y = mx + b to find b.

Worked Example

Data: Study time (h): 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6 — Test scores (%): 52, 60, 68, 75, 81, 90

Line of best fit passes through approximately (1, 52) and (6, 90).

m = (90 − 52)/(6 − 1) = 38/5 = 7.6

52 = 7.6(1) + b → b = 44.4

Equation: y = 7.6x + 44.4

Predict score for 3.5 hours: y = 7.6(3.5) + 44.4 = 71.0% (interpolation — within the 1–6 h range)

Predict score for 10 hours: y = 7.6(10) + 44.4 = 120.4% — unreliable extrapolation (score > 100% is impossible)

Correlation ≠ Causation

Two variables correlating strongly does NOT mean one causes the other. A third variable (confounder) often drives both. Always consider alternative explanations before claiming a causal relationship.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistakes
MistakeFix
Line must pass through the originThe line of best fit can have any y-intercept
Line of best fit must touch the most pointsEqual numbers above and below — trend matters, not touching points
Strong correlation proves causationCorrelation is one piece of evidence; controlled experiments are needed
Extrapolation is as reliable as interpolationExtrapolation grows increasingly uncertain beyond the data range
