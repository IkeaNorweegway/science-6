Similarity and Scale — Concept Summary

Reference sheet

Key Vocabulary

TermMeaningExample
Similar (~)Same shape, proportional sides, equal corresponding angles△ABC ~ △DEF
Congruent (≅)Same shape AND same size (scale factor = 1)
Scale factorRatio of image length to original lengthImage 8 cm, original 4 cm → k = 2
Corresponding sidesSides in matching positions between similar figuresAB corresponds to DE
Corresponding anglesAngles in matching positionsAngle A corresponds to angle D
ProportionEquation stating two ratios are equalAB/DE = BC/EF

Conditions for Similarity

Two polygons are similar if AND ONLY IF:
  1. All corresponding angles are equal, AND
  2. All corresponding sides are proportional

For triangles only — three shortcuts

CriterionWhat to check
AA (Angle-Angle)Two pairs of equal angles
SSSAll three side ratios equal
SASTwo sides proportional AND the included angle equal

AA is the most commonly used: if two angles match, the third must also (angles sum to 180°).

Scale Factor Rules
  • Scale factor k = image side / original side
  • All sides scale by k
  • Perimeters scale by k
  • Areas scale by k² (not tested in Grade 9 but a useful prediction)

Worked Examples

Worked Example 1 — Finding missing sides
△ABC ~ △DEF. AB = 6, BC = 9, AC = 12. DE = 4.

Scale factor = DE/AB = 4/6 = 2/3
EF = BC × (2/3) = 9 × (2/3) = 6
DF = AC × (2/3) = 12 × (2/3) = 8
Worked Example 2 — Indirect measurement (shadow method)
Measuring stick: 1.5 m tall, shadow 0.9 m. Tree shadow: 6 m. Find tree height.

h/6 = 1.5/0.9 (same time → same sun angle → similar triangles)
h = 6 × (1.5/0.9) = 10 m

The triangles are similar by AA: both have a right angle (vertical object) and the same sun angle.
Worked Example 3 — Scale diagram
Map scale 1 : 50 000. Two points are 3.6 cm apart on the map.

Real distance = 3.6 × 50 000 = 180 000 cm = 1.8 km

Common Mistakes

Mistakes → Fixes
MistakeFix
Similar = congruentSimilar means proportional, NOT equal in size
Setting up ratio the wrong wayMatch image to image, original to original: DE/AB = EF/BC
Using only one pair of equal angles to conclude SSSAA needs two angle pairs. SSS needs three side ratios.
Measuring shadows at different timesSun angle must be constant — measure simultaneously
