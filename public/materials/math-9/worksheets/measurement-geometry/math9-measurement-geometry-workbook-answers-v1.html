Q1 ★ — Circle vocabulary

Top diagram (triangle POP is inscribed in circle):

  • OP = radius
  • AB = diameter (passes through centre O)
  • PO extended beyond O to circle = diameter (the full line through O)
  • Angle POA (vertex at O) = central angle
  • Angle PAB (vertex at A, on circumference) = inscribed angle
  • Arc PB (shorter arc between P and B) = (minor) arc

Bottom diagram:

  • T = point of tangency
  • Line L = tangent

Q4 ★ — Chord + Pythagorean theorem

Chord PQ=20 cm, radius=13 cm.

(a) PM:
Perpendicular from centre bisects chord → PM = 20/2 = 10 cm because the perpendicular from the centre to any chord cuts it into two equal halves.

(b) OM:
Right triangle OMP: OA=13 (hypotenuse), PM=10, OM=?
OM = √(132−102) = √(169−100) = √69 ≈ 8.3 cm

(Note: √69 is irrational — not a Pythagorean triple. Correct to 1 decimal.)

Q5 ★ — Cylinder volume (Fort McMurray)

r=6, h=12.

(a) V = π(36)(12) = 432π ≈ 1357.17 m3

(b) 432π × 1000 ≈ 1,357,168 L

(c) 3/4 full: 1,357,168 × 3/4 ≈ 1,017,876 L (≈ 1.0 million litres)

Q6 ★ — Inscribed angle theorem

Central angle AOB = 96°.

(a) APB = 96° ÷ 2 = 48°

(b) AQB = 48° — All inscribed angles subtending the same arc are equal (each is ½ the same central angle).

(c) AB=diameter → APB = 90° (angle in a semicircle; central angle=180°, so inscribed=90°)

Q8 ★★ — SA vs V error analysis

Cylinder r=4, h=10.

(a) SA:
SA = 2π(16)+2π(4)(10) = 32π+80π = 112π ≈ 351.9 cm2
Not approximately 251 — closer to 352.

(b) V:
V = π(16)(10) = 160π ≈ 502.7 cm3
Not approximately 251 — closer to 503.

(c) Error:
The student conflated two fundamentally different measurements. SA measures total outer area (cm2) — amount of metal needed. V measures interior capacity (cm3) — amount it holds. They have different units, different meanings, and different values. Even if the numbers appeared similar, they couldn't "be the same thing" — one is 2D, the other is 3D.

Q9 ★ — Similar triangles, AA

DE∥BC, AD=3, DB=5, DE=4.

(a) Why similar:
Angle A is shared. DE∥BC → angle ADE=angle ABC and angle AED=angle ACB (corresponding angles). AA criterion.

(b) Scale factor:
AD/AB = 3/(3+5) = 3/8

(c) BC:
DE/BC = 3/8 → 4/BC = 3/8 → BC = 4 × 8/3 = 32/3 ≈ 10.7

Q10 ★ — Triangular prism SA

Equilateral base side=6, h=5.2, prism h=10.

(a) One triangular face = (6×5.2)/2 = 15.6 cm2
(b) Perimeter = 18 cm
(c) Lateral area = 18×10 = 180 cm2
(d) Total SA = 2(15.6)+180 = 31.2+180 = 211.2 cm2

Q11 ★ — Tangent-radius

Tangent TP=24, radius OT=7.

Right angle at T (tangent ⊥ radius). PO is hypotenuse.
PO = √(242+72) = √(576+49) = √625 = 25 cm

(5-7-24-25 isn't a standard triple, but this works: 576+49=625=252.)

Q12 ★★ — Shadow method (Calgary grain elevator)

Stick 1.5 m, shadow 0.9 m. Elevator shadow 22.5 m.

Setup: h/22.5 = 1.5/0.9 = 5/3
h = 22.5 × 5/3 = 37.5 m

Both shadows must be measured simultaneously — sun angle changes throughout the day, so the triangles are only similar at the same moment.

Q14 ★★ — Circle geometry, multi-step

Central AOB = 120°.

(a) APB = 120°/2 = 60°

(b) Major arc central = 240°. Inscribed = 120°

(c) 60° + 120° = 180° ✓

They always sum to 180° because the two arcs together form the full circle (360°). Their central angles sum to 360°. Each inscribed angle is half its central angle: ½(360°) = 180°. ✓

(c) Perimeter EFGH: 60 cm
(d) Ratio: 60/20 = 3 = scale factor ✓

Q16 ★ — Sphere scaling

r=3: SA=36π ≈ 113.10 m2. V=36π ≈ 113.10 m3.

r=6 (doubled):
SA2 = 4π(36) = 144π. Ratio = 144/36 = 4 = 22 ✓
V2 = (4/3)π(216) = 288π. Ratio = 288/36 = 8 = 23 ✓

Prediction: SA × 4, V × 8.

Q17 ★★ — Equal chords

Chords PQ=RS=16, radius=10.

Half-chord = 8. d2+64=100. d2=36. d = 6 cm for each chord.
Property 2: both equal chords are equidistant — no calculation needed. They're both 6 cm from centre.
Chord TU=8: half=4. d2=100-16=84. d=√84≈9.2 cm. Farther (shorter chord is farther from centre). ✓
