Cover the answer. Try the question. Then check.

1. SA and V

(a) Rectangular prism l=6, w=4, h=3:
SA = 2(lw+lh+wh) = 2(24+18+12) = 108 cm²
V = 6×4×3 = 72 cm³
(b) Cylinder r=5, h=10:
SA = 2π(25)+2π(5)(10) = 50π+100π = 150π ≈ 471.24 m²
V = π(25)(10) = 250π ≈ 785.40 m³

2. Net — rectangular prism 5×3×2 cm:

Two (5×3) = 30 | Two (5×2) = 20 | Two (3×2) = 12
SA = 62 cm²

3. Cone — find slant height first

(a) r=4, h=3:
l = √(16+9) = √25 = 5 cm
SA = π(4)(5)+π(16) = 20π+16π = 36π ≈ 113.10 cm²
V = (1/3)π(16)(3) = 16π ≈ 50.27 cm³
(b) r=6, h=8:
l = √(36+64) = √100 = 10 m
SA = π(6)(10)+π(36) = 60π+36π = 96π ≈ 301.59 m²
V = (1/3)π(36)(8) = 96π ≈ 301.59 m³

4. Sphere

(a) r=3 cm:
SA = 4π(9) = 36π ≈ 113.10 cm²
V = (4/3)π(27) = 36π ≈ 113.10 cm³
(b) r=7 m:
SA = 4π(49) = 196π ≈ 615.75 m²
V = (4/3)π(343) = (1372/3)π ≈ 1436.76 m³
(c) SA = 100π — find r:
4πr² = 100π → r² = 25 → r = 5 cm

5. Square-based pyramid — base 8×8, h=3, l=5

(a) Volume (use perpendicular height):
V = (1/3)(64)(3) = 64 m³
(b) Surface area (use slant height):
SA = 8² + 2(8)(5) = 64+80 = 144 m²

6. SA or Volume?

(a) Paint a cylindrical tank (r=2, h=5):
SA (coating = surface). SA = 2π(4)+2π(2)(5) = 8π+20π = 28π ≈ 87.96 m²
(b) Water in spherical tank (r=1.5 m):
V (capacity). V = (4/3)π(3.375) = 4.5π ≈ 14.14 m³
(c) Cardboard cereal box (20×7×30 cm):
SA (material). SA = 2(140)+2(600)+2(210) = 280+1200+420 = 1900 cm²

7. Composite — cylinder (r=3, h=8) on square prism (10×10×4)

(a) Volume:
V = (10)(10)(4) + π(9)(8) = 400+72π ≈ 626.12 cm³
(b) Hidden face:
The circular base of the cylinder (where it sits on the prism top) = π(9) = 9π cm²

(c) Outer surfaces included:

  • From prism: bottom (100 cm²), 4 lateral sides (4×40=160 cm²), top minus circle (100−9π)
  • From cylinder: lateral (2π×3×8=48π), top circle (9π)

8. Alberta grain bin — r=4, h=10

(a) Volume:
V = π(16)(10) = 160π ≈ 502.65 m³
Mass ≈ 160π × 700 ≈ 351,858 kg ≈ 352 tonnes
(b) Sheet metal (bottom + curved sides, no top):
SA = πr²+2πrh = π(16)+2π(4)(10) = 16π+80π = 96π ≈ 301.59 m²

9. Find the missing dimension

(a) Cylinder V=150π, r=5 — find h:
π(25)h = 150π → h = 6 cm
(b) Rectangular prism V=360, l=10, w=6 — find h:
10×6×h = 360 → 60h = 360 → h = 6 cm

10. Compare two containers

(a) Volume:
VA = π(25)(8) = 200π ≈ 628.32 cm³
VB = 8×8×10 = 640 cm³
Container B is larger (640 > 628.32)
(b) Surface area:
SAA = 2π(25)+2π(5)(8) = 50π+80π = 130π ≈ 408.41 cm²
SAB = 2(64)+4(8×10) = 128+320 = 448 cm²
Container B has greater SA (448 > 408.41)

Note: It's common for cylinders and rectangular prisms of similar volume to have very different surface areas — shape matters for packaging efficiency.
