1. Error analysis.

(a) A student says: "Any angle inscribed in a circle is 90°, because that's the inscribed angle theorem."

What did the student misremember? State the correct theorem precisely and give the specific condition under which 90° is correct.

(b) A student calculates the distance from the centre to a chord: chord = 20 cm, radius = 9 cm.

"Half-chord = 10 cm. OM = √(9² − 10²) = √(81 − 100) — this is negative, which is impossible."

Explain what this negative result means geometrically. Restate the problem with a valid radius.

2. Multi-step chord problem.

A circle has centre O and radius 17 cm. Two chords AB and CD are both 30 cm long.

(a) Find the distance from the centre to each chord.

(b) A third chord EF has length 16 cm. Find its distance from the centre and compare to AB's distance. Which chord is closer to the centre? Explain why this makes sense geometrically.

(c) What is the maximum possible length of a chord in this circle? What is its name and its distance from the centre?

3. Inscribed angle — multi-step.

In circle O, central angle AOB = 4x + 20°. Inscribed angle APB = x + 20°. Another inscribed angle AQB also subtends arc AB.

(a) Write and solve an equation to find x.

(b) Find central angle AOB and inscribed angle APB.

(c) Find inscribed angle AQB. Explain why it equals APB without additional calculation.

(d) Find the inscribed angle on the major arc. Show the calculation.

4. Tangent from external point — full analysis.

From external point P, two tangents PA and PB touch a circle with centre O and radius 5 cm. OP = 13 cm.

(a) Find the tangent length PA and PB.

(b) Find angle OAP. Explain why.

(c) The quadrilateral OAPB has angles at A and B equal to 90°. What do the four angles of OAPB sum to? Use this to find angle AOB + angle APB.

(d) If angle APB = 67°, find angle AOB.

5. Constructing the centre.

You are given a circle with no centre marked. Describe (without diagrams) a method using only a ruler and the chord properties to locate the exact centre. Your method must be precise enough that someone else could follow it.

6. Combining properties.

In circle O, chord AB = 24 cm and is 5 cm from the centre. Chord CD is 13 cm from the centre.

(a) Find the radius using chord AB.

(b) Find the length of chord CD.

(c) An inscribed angle APB subtends chord AB from the major arc. Find angle APB.

(Hint: You need the central angle first. Use the arc fraction: what fraction of the full circle does arc AB represent based on the central angle?)

7. Explain why, using the inscribed angle theorem.

Two students sit at different points P and Q on the same side of a chord AB, both on the circle. They measure their angles to the chord. Without any calculation, explain why their angles APB and AQB must be equal.
