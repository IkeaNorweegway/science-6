Circle Geometry — Concept Summary

Reference sheet

Key Vocabulary

TermMeaning
Centre (O)The point equidistant from all points on the circle
Radius (r)Segment from centre to any point on the circle
Diameter (d)Longest chord; passes through the centre; d = 2r
ChordSegment with both endpoints on the circle (not necessarily through centre)
ArcPart of the circle between two points
Central angleAngle with vertex at the centre
Inscribed angleAngle with vertex on the circle, sides are chords
TangentLine touching the circle at exactly one point
Point of tangencyThe single point where tangent meets circle

The Four Properties

Property 1 — Perpendicular from centre bisects the chord

If OM ⊥ chord AB, then AM = MB.

Converse: If OM bisects AB, then OM ⊥ AB.

Property 2 — Equal chords are equidistant from the centre

If chord PQ = chord RS (in the same circle), then their distances from the centre are equal.

Longer chord = closer to centre. Diameter = closest (distance 0).

Property 3 — Inscribed Angle Theorem

Inscribed angle = ½ × central angle (subtending the same arc)

Special case: If the chord is a diameter, the inscribed angle = 90°.

Property 4 — Tangent-Radius

At the point of tangency, the tangent ⊥ radius. This creates a right angle for Pythagorean theorem use.

Worked Examples

Worked Example 1 — Chord + Pythagorean theorem
Chord AB = 24 cm. Radius = 13 cm. Find the distance from centre O to the chord.

OM ⊥ AB → AM = 12 cm (half the chord)
Right triangle OMA: OA = 13, AM = 12, OM = ?
OM = √(13² − 12²) = √(169 − 144) = √25 = 5 cm
Worked Example 2 — Inscribed angle theorem
Central angle AOB = 110°. Find the inscribed angle APB.

APB = 110°/2 = 55°

Inscribed angle AQB on the major arc (arc = 360° − 110° = 250°):
AQB = 250°/2 = 125°
Worked Example 3 — Tangent length
Tangent PT from external point P. Radius OT = 6 cm. OP = 10 cm.

Right angle at T (tangent ⊥ radius)
PT = √(OP² − OT²) = √(100 − 36) = √64 = 8 cm

Common Mistakes
