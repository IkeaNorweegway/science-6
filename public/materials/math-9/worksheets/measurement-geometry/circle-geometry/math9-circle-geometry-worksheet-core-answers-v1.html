Cover the answer. Try the question. Then check.

1. Labelling the circle

  • P is on the circumference (circle)
  • OA is a radius
  • AB is the diameter
  • Arc PB is an arc
  • Angle POB (vertex at O): central angle
  • Angle PAB (vertex at A, on the circle): inscribed angle
  • T is the point of tangency
  • Line L is a tangent

2. Chord properties

(a) Chord AB = 30 cm, OM ⊥ AB — find AM:
Property: Perpendicular from centre bisects the chord
AM = 30/2 = 15 cm
(b) Radius = 10 cm, chord 12 cm from centre — is this possible?
A chord 12 cm from the centre cannot exist in a circle with radius 10 cm — the perpendicular distance to any chord cannot exceed the radius. This configuration is impossible.

Recalculate with chord 6 cm from centre:
Half-chord = √(10²−6²) = √(100−36) = √64 = 8 cm
Full chord = 16 cm
(c) Two chords equidistant from centre, PQ = 18 cm — find RS:
Property: Equal distances from centre → equal chord lengths
RS = 18 cm

3. Inscribed angle theorem

(a) Central angle AOB = 76°:
Inscribed angle = ½ × central
APB = 38°
(b) Inscribed angle APB = 33°:
Central angle = 2 × inscribed
AOB = 66°
(c) AB is a diameter — find angle APB:
APB = 90°
Why: AB is a diameter, so the central angle = 180° (straight line). Inscribed angle = 180°/2 = 90°. This is the "angle in a semicircle" theorem.
(d) Central angle AOB = 150° — find inscribed angle on major arc:
Major arc central = 360°−150° = 210°
Inscribed angle on major arc = 210°/2 = 105°

4. Tangent-radius problems

(a) OT = 5 cm, OP = 13 cm:
Right angle at T (tangent ⊥ radius)
PT = √(13²−5²) = √(169−25) = √144 = 12 cm
(b) Radius = 8 m, external point = 17 m from centre:
PT = √(17²−8²) = √(289−64) = √225 = 15 m
(c) Two tangents from P, PA = 11 cm — find PB:
PB = 11 cm
Reason: Tangent segments from the same external point are equal in length.

5. Full chord problem — radius 10, chord AB = 16 cm

(a) Distance from O to AB:
Half-chord = 8 cm
OM = √(10²−8²) = √(100−64) = √36 = 6 cm
(b) Second chord CD = 16 cm — distance from centre?
CD is 6 cm from the centre.
Property applied: Equal chords are equidistant from the centre (AB = CD = 16 cm → same distance).

6. Finding x — central and inscribed angles

Given: Central angle AOB = 3x+10°; Inscribed angle APB = x+15°

Setting up: inscribed = ½ × central
x + 15 = ½(3x+10) = 1.5x + 5
10 = 0.5x
x = 20

Central angle = 3(20)+10 = 70°
Inscribed angle = 20+15 = 35°
Check: 35 = 70/2 ✓

7. True or False

(a) Any angle inscribed in a circle equals 90°: FALSE
Correction: Only inscribed angles that subtend a diameter (a semicircle) equal 90°. In general, inscribed angle = ½ × central angle for the same arc.

(b) A chord that passes through the centre is a diameter: TRUE

(c) The inscribed angle is half the central angle for the same arc: TRUE

(d) From any external point, both tangent segments to the circle are equal: TRUE

8. Calgary skating rink — diameter 40 m, chord 10 m from centre

(a) Radius: 40/2 = 20 m

(b) Half the chord length:
√(r²−d²) = √(20²−10²) = √(400−100) = √300 = 10√3 ≈ 17.3 m

(c) Full chord length:
2 × 10√3 = 20√3 ≈ 34.6 m (exact: 20√3 m)
