Vocabulary

TermDefinitionExample
Linear relationA relationship where equal changes in x produce equal changes in y; graphs as a straight liney = 3x + 2
Table of valuesA chart listing specific (x, y) pairsx: 0,1,2 and y: 2,5,8
Independent variableThe variable you choose/control; the input; usually xTime, number of items
Dependent variableThe variable that depends on the input; the output; usually yCost, distance
EquationAn algebraic rule that gives y for any value of xy = −2x + 5
Rule of FourFour equivalent ways to represent the same relation: verbal, table, equation, graphAll four show the same relationship

Section 2 — Slope as Rate of Change

Vocabulary

TermDefinitionExample
SlopeMeasure of steepness and direction of a line; ratio of rise to runm = 3 means 3 units up per 1 unit right
RiseVertical change; change in y (y₂ − y₁)If y goes from 2 to 8, rise = 6
RunHorizontal change; change in x (x₂ − x₁)If x goes from 1 to 4, run = 3
Rate of changeSlope applied to a real-world context; how much y changes per unit of x100 km/h means 100 km per hour
Positive slopeLine rises from left to rightm = 2
Negative slopeLine falls from left to rightm = −3
Zero slopeHorizontal line; y doesn't changey = 5
Undefined slopeVertical line; x doesn't change (division by zero)x = 3

Unit Synthesis

Part A — Key ideas

QuestionAnswer
1. Four representationsVerbal (meaning in words), Table (specific values), Equation (general algebraic rule), Graph (visual shape and direction)
2. Slope formulam = (y₂ − y₁) / (x₂ − x₁)
3. m and b in y=mx+bm = slope (steepness and direction); b = y-intercept (starting value where line crosses y-axis)
4. Direct vs. partialDirect: y=kx, passes through origin, y/x constant. Partial: y=kx+b (b≠0), doesn't pass through origin. From table: x=0 gives y=0 → direct; y≠0 → partial.
