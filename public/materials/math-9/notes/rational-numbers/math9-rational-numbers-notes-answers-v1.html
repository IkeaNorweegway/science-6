Unit Synthesis

Part A — Key ideas

QuestionModel answer
1. What makes a number rational? Two non-obvious examples.Any number expressible as a/b (integers, b ≠ 0). E.g., √4 = 2 = 2/1 is rational; 0.666... = 2/3 is rational.
2. Common denominator for addition but not multiplication?Addition: denominators name the size of pieces — you can't add different-sized pieces. Multiplication: you multiply the pieces themselves; the size relationship doesn't need to match.
3. Why does dividing by a fraction < 1 give a larger result?You're asking "how many of this small piece fit?" Since the piece is smaller than 1, more fit — the count exceeds what you started with.
4. Sign rule for neg × negNegative × negative = positive (even count of negatives → positive product)
