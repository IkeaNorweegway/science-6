Central vs inscribed angle:

  • Central: vertex at centre
  • Inscribed: vertex on the circle
  • Arc intercepted: lies inside/between the angle's sides

Tangent: exactly one point. Perpendicular to the radius at that point.

Tangent ≠ chord: tangent meets circle at one point (one endpoint ON circle, extends to external); chord has BOTH endpoints on circle.

Arc types: minor = shorter arc; major = longer arc; semicircle = exactly half the circle, cut by a diameter

Unit Synthesis

Part A — Key ideas

QuestionAnswer
1. Two chord properties(1) A perpendicular from the centre bisects the chord. (2) Equal chords are equidistant from the centre (and converse).
2. Inscribed angle theorem + special caseInscribed angle = ½ × central angle (same arc). Special case: when chord is a diameter (central angle = 180°), inscribed angle = 90°.
3. Tangent-radius + Pythagorean theoremTangent is perpendicular to radius at point of tangency. This creates a right angle at T, forming a right triangle (centre-tangency-external point) with the external-to-centre segment as hypotenuse.
