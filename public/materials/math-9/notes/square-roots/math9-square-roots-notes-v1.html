Guided Notes

Most numbers are NOT perfect squares.

√3, √7, √10 are not integers. They are not fractions either. They are numbers — they cannot be written as a/b where a and b are integers.

What does irrational mean?

  • The decimal expansion never terminates and never repeats
  • √2 = 1.41421356... (goes on forever with no repeating block)
  • It exists on the number line — it has a location — but cannot be expressed as a ratio
Misconception

Wrong idea: "√2 must equal some fraction like 139/98 or something — we just haven't found it yet."

Why it fails: It has been proven (since ancient Greece) that √2 cannot equal any fraction a/b. If you assume it does and simplify, you reach a contradiction. This is not a matter of precision — it is literally impossible to write √2 as a fraction, no matter how many decimal places you use.

Correct: √2 is irrational. 1.41421... is just the decimal approximation, not the exact value. The only exact form is √2.
