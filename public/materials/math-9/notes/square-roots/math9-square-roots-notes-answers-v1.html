Unit Synthesis

Part A — Key ideas

QuestionModel answer
1. Perfect vs. non-perfect squarePerfect: result of squaring an integer (e.g., 49 = 72). Non-perfect: positive integer not the square of any integer (e.g., 50).
2. Why is √2 irrational?Its decimal expansion never terminates and never repeats — it has been mathematically proven impossible to write √2 as any fraction a/b, no matter how precise.
3. Pythagorean theorema2 + b2 = c2, where a, b are the legs and c is the hypotenuse (side opposite the right angle).
4. x(1/2) equals? Why?√x. Because x(1/2) × x(1/2) = x1, and √x × √x = x — they perform the same operation.
