Misconception

−3⁴ is not the same as (−3)⁴

Wrong idea: "−3⁴ and (−3)⁴ both equal +81 because a negative times a negative is positive."

Misconception

a⁰ is not zero

Wrong idea: "Anything to the power of zero is zero, because you're multiplying nothing."

Why it fails: An exponent does not mean "multiply by zero" — it tells you how many times to use the base as a factor. The pattern above shows that each time you decrease the exponent by 1, you divide by the base. Starting from a¹ = a and dividing by a gives a ÷ a = 1, not 0.

One important exception: What about 0⁰?

The pattern breaks here. If we apply our rule, we would say 0⁰ = 1. But 0¹ ÷ 0 is division by zero, which is undefined. For this course, we will say: 0⁰ is undefined. The rule a⁰ = 1 only applies when a ≠ 0.

Misconception

Product rule vs. Power of a Power rule

Wrong idea: 2³ × 2⁴ = 2¹² because "you multiply exponents when powers are involved."

Why it fails: These are different operations.

  • Multiplying two powers (same base): 2³ × 2⁴ → add the exponents → 2⁷
  • Raising a power to a power: (2³)⁴ → multiply the exponents → 2¹²

The operation that connects the two powers tells you what to do with the exponents.

Misconception

The power does NOT distribute over addition

Wrong idea: (a + b)² = a² + b²

Why it fails: Expand it: (a + b)² = (a + b)(a + b) = a² + 2ab + b². The middle term 2ab appears because both factors multiply each other. Distributing an exponent only works when the factors inside are connected by multiplication or division, not addition or subtraction.

Misconception

A negative exponent does not mean a negative number

Wrong idea: "2⁻³ = −8 because the negative makes it negative."

Why it fails: The negative exponent is an instruction about position, not sign. It means "move to the denominator and make the exponent positive." 2⁻³ = 1/2³ = 1/8, which is a small positive fraction, not a negative number.

Can a power with a negative exponent ever be negative? Yes — but only if the base is negative. (−2)⁻³ = 1/(−2)³ = 1/(−8) = −1/8.
