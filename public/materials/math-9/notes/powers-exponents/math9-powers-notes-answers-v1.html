Unit Synthesis

Reconstructed laws:

RuleSymbol form
Product of powersam × an = am+n
Quotient of powersam ÷ an = am−n
Power of a power(am)n = amn
Power of a product(ab)n = anbn
Zero exponenta0 = 1
Negative exponenta−n = 1/an

Model explanations (any two):

  • Product rule: Expanding shows 3 twos × 4 twos = 7 twos total. Adding exponents counts the combined factors correctly.
  • Zero exponent: Each step down divides by the base. Starting at a1 = a, dividing by a gives a/a = 1. The pattern must give 1, not 0.
  • Negative exponent: Quotient rule applied to a smaller top: a2 ÷ a5 = a−3. Expanding gives 1/a3. So a−n = 1/an — an instruction to invert, not to negate.
  • Power of product vs. sum: Multiplication lets us regroup factors freely; (ab)2 = a2b2 by regrouping. Addition cannot be regrouped the same way — (a+b)2 must be expanded as (a+b)(a+b), producing the cross-term 2ab.
