Unit Synthesis — Answer Key

Part A

QuestionAnswer
1. Theoretical probability formulaP(event) = number of favourable outcomes / total outcomes
2. Multiplication rulesIndependent: P(A and B) = P(A)×P(B). Dependent: P(A then B) = P(A)×P(B|A)
3. Gambler's FallacyBelieving past independent events affect future probability. Example: "I've been dealt bad cards — I must be due for a good hand."
4. Dependent vs. independentKey question: does the first outcome change what's available for the second? Without replacement → dependent. With replacement or separate events → independent.

1. P(heads) = 1/2 — always, regardless of past flips. The Gambler's Fallacy.

2. Theoretical: from structure (equally likely). Experimental: from trials. They converge with large numbers of trials.
