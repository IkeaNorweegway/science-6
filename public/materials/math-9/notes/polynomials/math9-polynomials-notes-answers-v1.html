Unit Synthesis

Part A — Key Ideas

QuestionAnswer
1. Degree and how to find itThe degree of the highest-exponent term. Find by inspecting each term's exponent and taking the largest.
2. Why can't you add 3x² and 2x?They have different exponents (2 vs 1) — unlike terms. Like adding apples to oranges; the variable parts don't match.
3. Most important step in subtractionDistribute the negative sign to every term in the subtracted polynomial — not just the first.
4. Exponent rule for multiplying same-base powersxm × xn = x(m+n). Example: x²×x³ = x⁵.
5. How many partial products for (ax+b)(cx+d)?Always 4 — every term in first (2) times every term in second (2) = 2×2 = 4.
