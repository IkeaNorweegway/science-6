Answer Key — Not for Distribution

Section 1

1. P(red) = 3/8 = 37.5%. P(not red) = 5/8 = 62.5%.

2. Theoretical probability = calculated from equally likely outcomes (what should happen). Experimental probability = calculated from actual results (what did happen). Getting 8/20 = 0.40 vs. theoretical 3/8 = 0.375 is not a problem — experimental probability approaches theoretical probability as the number of trials increases, but will not match exactly with small samples.

Section 2

1. 2 (coin outcomes) × 6 (die outcomes) = 12 outcomes in sample space. P(heads and 4) = 1/12.

2. If you only count favorable outcomes (e.g., just the "heads and 4" outcomes), you have no way to form the denominator of your probability fraction. The denominator must represent all equally likely outcomes. Listing only favorable outcomes could lead you to write a probability greater than 1 or miscalculate the likelihood.

Section 3

1. P(red) = 4/10 = 2/5. P(blue) = 6/10 = 3/5 (same each draw because marble is replaced). P(red then blue) = 2/5 × 3/5 = 6/25.

2. Independent events: the outcome of the first event does not affect the probability of the second event. Replacing the marble restores the original bag — the second draw has the same 4 red / 6 blue composition as the first. Without replacement: one marble is gone, so both the number of marbles and the composition change — the events become dependent.

Section 4

1. P(red on 1st) = 4/10. After drawing red, bag has 3 red + 6 blue = 9 marbles. P(blue on 2nd | red on 1st) = 6/9 = 2/3. P(red then blue) = 4/10 × 6/9 = 24/90 = 4/15.

2. After the first draw (without replacement): the total number of marbles decreases by 1 (from 10 to 9), AND the composition changes depending on what colour was drawn. Without replacement: if you drew red, P(blue on 2nd) = 6/9. With replacement: P(blue on 2nd) = 6/10 regardless of what you drew first. The sample space shrinks and shifts when events are dependent.
