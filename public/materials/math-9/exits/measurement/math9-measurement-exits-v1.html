Answer Key — Not for Distribution

Section 1

1. Triangle: A = ½ × 9 × 6 = 27 cm². Circle (r = 5 cm): A = π × 5² = 25π cm².

2. Surface area is the total area of all the flat (and curved) faces of a 3D object. To find each face's area, you use the appropriate 2D formula (rectangle, triangle, circle, etc.) and sum them all.

Section 2

1. Three pairs of opposite faces: top/bottom (4×3 = 12 each), front/back (4×5 = 20 each), sides (3×5 = 15 each). SA = 2(12) + 2(20) + 2(15) = 24 + 40 + 30 = 94 cm².

2. A triangular prism has 5 faces: 2 triangular bases + 3 rectangular lateral faces. A cylinder's curved surface unrolls into a rectangle because the height stays constant — the width of that rectangle is the circumference of the circular base (2πr), and the height is the cylinder's height.

Section 3

1. V = π × r² × h = π × 9 × 8 = 72π cm³

2. Each "layer" of the prism (parallel slice) is congruent to the base. Stacking those identical layers gives volume = base area × number of layers = base area × height. The height represents how many layers deep the object is — the perpendicular distance between the two bases.

Section 4

1. V = (1/3)πr²h = (1/3) × π × 16 × 9 = 48π cm³

2. The pyramid's volume is 1/3 of the prism's volume. Physical reasoning: three identical pyramids with the same base and height can be rearranged to exactly fill one rectangular prism — this is demonstrable by cutting a cube into 3 equal pyramids.

Section 5

1. Cylinder: V = π × 9 × 10 = 90π m³. Cone: V = (1/3) × π × 9 × 4 = 12π m³. Total: 102π m³.

2. You do NOT include: the top circle of the cylinder (it is hidden where the cone sits on top) and the base circle of the cone (it is the same shared face). If calculating exterior surface area, also include the base of the cylinder (bottom of the silo). You only count exposed surfaces.
