Answer Key — Not for Distribution

Section 1

1. Rational: √9 (= 3), −5, 0.666..., 0, −3/4   Not rational: π, √2

2. Least to greatest: −3/4, −1/3, 0, 1/2
Explanation: For negative fractions, the one with the larger absolute value is smaller (farther left on the number line). −3/4 = −0.75 < −1/3 ≈ −0.33.

Section 2

1. −2/3 + (−1/4): LCD = 12. −8/12 + (−3/12) = −11/12

2. Error: student added numerators (1+2=3) and added denominators (3+2=5) — denominators name piece sizes, you can't add different-sized pieces directly.
Correct: 1/3 = 2/6, 1/2 = 3/6 → 2/6 + 3/6 = 5/6

Section 3

1. (−3/4) × (8/9): cross-cancel 3 and 9 (÷3) → (−1/4) × (8/3); cross-cancel 8 and 4 (÷4) → (−1/1) × (2/3) = −2/3

2. Two negatives → even number of negatives → positive sign. Result: (2/5) × (3/7) × (1/2) = 6/70 = 3/35

Section 4

1. (−5/6) ÷ (2/3) = (−5/6) × (3/2) = −15/12 = −5/4 (or −1¼)

2. Dividing by 1/4 asks "how many quarter-pieces fit in 6?" → many more than 6. 6 ÷ (1/4) = 6 × 4 = 24. Dividing by a fraction less than 1 multiplies by something greater than 1.

Section 5

1. (−1/2 + 1/4) × (−8): Bracket first: −2/4 + 1/4 = −1/4. Then (−1/4) × (−8) = 8/4 = 2.

2. Student A is correct (BEDMAS: multiplication before addition). Student B violated the order of operations — addition comes after multiplication when there are no brackets.
