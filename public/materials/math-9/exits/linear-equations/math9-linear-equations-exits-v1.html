Answer Key — Not for Distribution

Section 1

1. 3x − 7 = 14 → 3x = 21 → x = 7

2. Inverse operation = the operation that "undoes" another (addition/subtraction, multiplication/division). Verify by substituting back: 3(7) − 7 = 21 − 7 = 14 ✓

Section 2

1. 6x − 3 = 2x + 9 → 4x = 12 → x = 3

2. 2x + 6 = 3x − 1 → collect variable terms on one side: 6 + 1 = 3x − 2x → x = 7. After expanding, you must move all variable terms to one side and all constants to the other before isolating x.

Section 3

1. LCD of 3 and 4 = 12. Multiply everything by 12: 4x + 3x = 84 → 7x = 84 → x = 12.

2. Multiplying by the LCD converts all fractions to integers, eliminating fraction arithmetic throughout. First step for (2/3)x − 1/2 = (5/6)x: multiply all terms by 6 → 4x − 3 = 5x → x = −3.

Section 4

1. −2x + 5 > 11 → −2x > 6 → x < −3 (sign flips when dividing by −2). Graph: open circle at −3, arrow pointing left.

2. True inequality example: 2 < 5. Multiply both sides by −1: −2 and −5. On the number line, −5 < −2, so the direction reverses. Multiplying by a negative reflects both values across zero, swapping their relative positions.

Section 5

1. Anika = 2j + 15. Equation: j + (2j + 15) = 105 → 3j + 15 = 105 → 3j = 90 → j = 30. Jamal = $30, Anika = $75.

2. (1) Verify: 30 + 75 = 105 ✓ and 75 = 2(30) + 15 = 75 ✓. (2) Answer in context with units: "Jamal has $30 and Anika has $75."
