Answer Key — Not for Distribution

Section 1

1. Degree = 2; coefficients = 3, −5; constant = 2. Trinomial (three terms).

2. Monomial = one term (e.g., 4x², degree 2). Binomial = two terms (e.g., 3x − 1, degree 1). Trinomial = three terms (e.g., x² + 2x − 5, degree 2).

Section 2

1. 4x² + 5x² = 9x²; −3x + 2x = −x; constant −1 unchanged. Result: 9x² − x − 1.

2. Error 1: added coefficients across unlike terms (3x² and 2x have different variable parts). Error 2: added the exponents (got x³). Like terms must have the same variable AND the same exponent. 3x² and 2x differ in their exponent, so they cannot be combined.

Section 3

1. (3x² − x²) + (2x + 5x) + (−4 + 3) = 2x² + 7x − 1

2. You can only combine like terms (same variable, same exponent). 3x² and 2x cannot be combined because they are different kinds of terms — x² represents an area and x represents a length; they are fundamentally different quantities.

Section 4

1. Distribute the negative: 4x² − 3x + 1 − 2x² − x + 5 = 2x² − 4x + 6

2. Correct: (5x² − 2x + 3) − (3x² + x − 4) = 5x² − 2x + 3 − 3x² − x + 4 = 2x² − 3x + 7. Student likely kept the −4 as negative instead of changing it to +4 when distributing the minus sign.

Section 5

1. 3x × 2x² = 6x³; 3x × (−4x) = −12x²; 3x × 1 = 3x. Result: 6x³ − 12x² + 3x

2. 2x(x + 3) − 4(x² − 1) = 2x² + 6x − 4x² + 4 = (2x² − 4x²) + 6x + 4 = −2x² + 6x + 4

Section 6

1. (x)(x) + (x)(−5) + (3)(x) + (3)(−5) = x² − 5x + 3x − 15 = x² − 2x − 15

2. Area model sub-areas: 2x·x = 2x², 2x·4 = 8x, 1·x = x, 1·4 = 4. Combined: 2x² + 9x + 4.
