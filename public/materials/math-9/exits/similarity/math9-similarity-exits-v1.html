Answer Key — Not for Distribution

Section 1

1. Cross-multiply: 5 × 36 = 12x → 180 = 12x → x = 15. Or: 36 ÷ 12 = 3, so 5 × 3 = 15.

2. Ratio: a comparison of two quantities with the same unit (e.g., 3 red marbles to 5 blue marbles). Rate: a comparison of two quantities with different units (e.g., 80 km/h, $3.50/L). A ratio has no units (or same units cancel); a rate always has units from two different measures.

Section 2

1. Check ratios: 6/3 = 2, 8/4 = 2, 10/5 = 2 — all equal. Similar: Yes. Scale factor: 2 (from A to B).

2. (1) All corresponding angles must be equal AND (2) all corresponding sides must be in the same ratio. With just equal angles, you know the shape but not the size. With just proportional sides, the angles could still vary (for polygons with 4+ sides). Both conditions together fully define similarity.

Section 3

1. AA (Angle-Angle). Third angles: since the three angles of any triangle sum to 180°, if two pairs are equal, the third pair must be equal too.

2. AA: requires proving only two angle pairs equal — often easier because angles can be identified from parallel lines, vertical angles, or shared angles. SSS: requires measuring/knowing all three side ratios — useful when angles aren't accessible but measurements are. Real-world: AA is useful in surveying — if you can identify two matching angles (from the sun's position or parallel lines), you can establish similarity without measuring all sides.

Section 4

1. 4.6 cm × 5 m/cm = 23 m.

2. Set up proportion: tree height/9 m = 1 m/1.5 m → tree height = 9/1.5 = 6 m. The triangles are similar by AA: both contain a right angle (objects are vertical, shadows are horizontal) and both experience the same sun angle at the same time of day.
